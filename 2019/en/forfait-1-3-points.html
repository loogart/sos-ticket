Important Information and Common Questions for Drivers Fighting a Stop Sign Ticket

How Should I Act When Getting a Ticket?

If you ever find yourself on the receiving end of a stop sign ticket in Montreal (or a ticket for another minor offence), there are four central tips we would recommend following.

  • Be polite to the police officer giving you the ticket. Being rude can only make it worse.
  • Don't admit guilt, even if you think you may be guilty of the traffic violation.
  • Don't pay the fine after coming home, since doing so would be a de facto admission of guilt.
  • Consult a legal expert at SOS Ticket before taking any concrete steps.

How Much Will a Stop Sign Ticket Cost Me?

If you fail to stop your car in time, you can expect to lose 3 demerit points for rolling through a stop sign, and you will also pay a penalty. If you fight the stop sign ticket in court and lose, it will cost you between $200 and $1,000. Once you have been convicted of this offence it will remain on your record for three years. To fight it, call us!

How Long Do I Have to Pay a Ticket in Quebec?

For any parking ticket, you have 30 days to pay. After that, late fees will begin to apply to your file. For other infractions, the length of time you have to pay will depend on whether or not you decide to plead guilty. If you are a driver that was accused of a moving violation and would like to prove that you are innocent, call the lawyers at SOS Ticket. We'll fight for you and do our best to prove you are a safe driver who should keep their demerit points.
