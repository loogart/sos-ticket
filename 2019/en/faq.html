Frequently Asked Questions

+ How to contest a ticket?

You can plead not guilty by responding to the form that was given to you with the ticket. You do not have to give justifications and it is even risky to do so because it could be used against you.

It is recommended that you write "I request disclosure of proof" on the form to obtain the proof against you in the file. This will facilitate the process later. You can transfer it to our representatives to read it when opening the file.

Make a copy of the form, send it to be received within 30 days of its issuance and wait for the Court's notice informing you of the time and place of your hearing.

+ How to contest a parking violation?

A ticket relating to parking may be contested by responding to the notice left on the windshield of your car. The section to complete allows you to check "guilty" or "not guilty", giving your explanations and sending it all to the municipal court concerned. Then you may have to justify yourself to a judge.

Although we would be pleased to assist you, we do not contest parking tickets for clients. From your point of view, considering the amount involved, it is better to answer for yourself than to mandate a lawyer.

+ What are the contesting fees?

Court fees are determined by regulation and indexed annually. If you are convicted by the judge after contesting, these costs are added to the fine:

+ How to contest an excessive speeding ticket?
A ticket for excessive speeding can be contested by first responding with a non-guilty plea. In this way, you declare that you are not guilty of what you are accused of. Once this step is completed, our lawyers can take charge of your file to determine the optimal strategy to proceed. Excessive speeding tickets are not easily negotiable, so a well-organized file needs to be prepared. Contact us for more information.

+ How to contest a ticket online?

A ticket needs to be contested with the provided form, in paper. The contest to a ticket, respond the notice given by the agent, where you have the option to declare that you are not guilty. Then the help of a legal advisor, as we offer to SOS Ticket, becomes useful. If you prefer to represent yourself alone to plead your case, this is possible. You will receive your court date and you will appear before the judge to give your arguments.

By calling us, we can take charge of your case, analyze your driving record and your offense and discuss with you the best options. Our years of experience and ongoing research allow us to look for results that make the difference on your license. Contact us, it's free and confidential.

+ Is it worth contesting a ticket?

It is worthwhile to contest a ticket only when there is a valid reason to believe that the law has not been respected, that the techniques used by the police officer are not in accordance with the law, or that you simply did not commit the offense for which you are charged. It is not every ticket that can be contested, and we do not encourage to do so unless the circumstances are appropriate. For example, the legal costs must be worth it in relation to the points and the fine that are at stake. Call us to find out more about your particular situation, it will be our pleasure to direct you.

+ Is it necessary to provide an explanation at the specified area on the ticket when pleading not guilty?

You do not have to give justifications and it is even risky to do so because it could be used against you.

+ Can you guarantee your results?

It is impossible to guarantee results. In general, in front of the legal system, since we are dealing with the law and its interpretation, the results cannot be guaranteed.

+ Is it possible to automatically successfully contest a ticket if I go to court while the police officer does not show up?

No! The prosecutor is the Crown. The police officer only writes the report, and this report has the same value as if the police officer repeated the same thing in court. You can request that the police officer be present, but it is not usually not recommended to do so as you may be ordered to pay additional fees if the judge decides it was not necessary

+ When do I have to go to court?

After you plead not guilty, you will receive (perhaps several months later) a "Notice of Hearing" giving you the date, time and place of your trial in court. + My fine is less expensive than your rates, why should I not just pay the fine?

The cost of the fine is only one of the consequences of the ticket, and often the most temporary. You will lose your points, and potentially even your license, your insurance will increase and the renewal fees of your driver's license may also increase. It is therefore important in certain cases to plead not guilty and to contest. Our lawyers will only take the necessary means to defend you at a fair and proportionate price

+ My driver's license was suspended due to the accumulation of demerit points, am I eligible for a restricted license?

Driving has to be necessary for the execution of your work, to qualify for the restricted license. For example, a pizza delivery person would be eligible.

+ How long do my offenses remain on my driver's license?

Two years from the time you plead guilty to the offenses. However, for excessive speeding, registration remains for 10 years from that time.
