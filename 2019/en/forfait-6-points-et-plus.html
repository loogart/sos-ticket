Common Questions

What Should I Do if I'm Involved in an Accident?

Millions of motor vehicle accidents happen every year across Canada and the United States. Luckily, most accidents only involve damage to property, such as other vehicles or your own. However, approximately one third of all accidents in Quebec do involve some form of personal injury.

If you've had an accident with your vehicle in Quebec, here are some important things you should do:

  • Stay there: Never leave the scene of an accident.
  • Call the police: Even when there appear to be no serious injuries, it's always a best practice to file a police report. This will help you with your insurance company later on. Provide the police with all the details you remember. Very often, injuries only become apparent after the accident, so if the police ask you if you are injured and you're not sure, tell them that.
  • Take pictures: Having an accurate record of the motor vehicle accident is a must. Make sure to capture all visible damage to any vehicles and record any possible physical injuries.
  • Share information: Make sure to collect the name and address as well as the contact information of any person involved in the motor vehicle accident. It's a good idea to record the contact information of nearby witnesses and other drivers as well.
  • Get medical attention: We advise that you seek medical attention no matter what. Since many injuries only become obvious hours or even days after the incident, you should make sure to get checked out by a doctor, even if you feel fine. It will help protect your rights and your wallet.
  • Report the accident to your insurance company: Many insurance companies expect you to contact them as soon as possible after an incident involving a vehicle. Provide the company with the police report number and any information about your medical condition. However, before you do....
  • Protect your rights: After you have taken the steps above, the most important thing you can do is to contact a lawyer. At SOS Ticket, we can assist you as you navigate the situation, providing advice about dealing with insurance companies, and helping you protect yourself from a legal perspective.

Can I Be Charged for Not Stopping at an Accident?

Whether the incident in question is a hit and run with another vehicle, rolling past a school bus without stopping, or something else relatively serious, any person will absolutely be charged if they do not stay at the scene. You will be charged either under the Criminal Code of Canada (or a similar provincial statute) for “failing to stop at the scene of the accident."
